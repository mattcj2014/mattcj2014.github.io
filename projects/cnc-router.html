Overview

This project involved the complete design and construction of a custom CNC router intended for woodworking applications. The goal was to achieve a rigid, accurate machine using a mix of aluminum extrusion, custom plates, and integrated electronics.

CAD Design

The machine was fully modeled in CAD prior to fabrication, allowing for interference checks, motion verification, and plate optimization.

Physical Build

The final machine closely follows the CAD model, with adjustments made during assembly to improve rigidity, cable management, and serviceability.

Electronics and Control

The router uses a GRBL based controller with external stepper drivers, limit switches, and an enclosed electronics box for safety and reliability.

Results and Lessons Learned

The completed machine achieved reliable and repeatable cuts in hardwoods. Through this build, I gained hands-on experience balancing rigidity, cost, and manufacturability in a real CNC system.
